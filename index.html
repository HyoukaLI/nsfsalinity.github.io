Background

Ocean drifters are pillars of modern marine observation

Ocean drifters provide continuous, real-time measurements across vast and often inaccessible regions of the ocean. They support the monitoring of essential oceanographic variables such as salinity, temperature, waves, currents, and atmosphere–ocean fluxes.

Compared with remote sensing, drifters offer in-situ validation and can capture fine-grained nearshore dynamics that are difficult to recover from low-resolution satellite products. At the same time, sparse tracks and heterogeneous ocean conditions make modeling and imputation highly challenging.

Problem Definition

Sea surface salinity imputation as tensor completion

We formulate salinity imputation under sparse drifter trajectories as a partially observed 4D tensor reconstruction problem over time, latitude, longitude, and sensor-variable dimensions.

Given sparse trajectories with observed salinity and auxiliary variables, the goal is to recover a fully dense salinity field for all spatial and temporal grid cells.
