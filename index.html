Background

Why sparse drifter trajectories matter for ocean salinity modeling

Ocean drifters offer a valuable way to observe real marine environments, but they usually sample only sparse trajectories rather than dense spatial fields. This creates a fundamental gap between what is observed and what ocean analysis or decision-making needs: a continuous understanding of salinity variation over space and time.

Research motivation

Salinity is a key indicator of ocean dynamics, freshwater transport, mixing processes, and coastal environmental change. In practice, drifter-based measurements are sparse, irregular, and strongly influenced by local currents, tides, and regional circulation.

The core task is therefore to infer dense salinity structure from limited moving observations. This requires a method that can combine local coastal patterns with broader regional relationships.
